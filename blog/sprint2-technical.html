.absolute-div {
 position: absolute;
top: 0%;
left: 25%;

I have been placed specifically here 0% from the top of the container (which is my parent) and 25% from the left. I am removed from the normal flow of the document.

Because of this, I can be placed infront of, or behind other elements such as divs without affecting their placement within the container.

.relative-div {
 position: relative;
top: 50%;
left: 50%;

I have been told to move away from my original position (the blue div) inside the document.

I am positioned offset 50% from the top, and 50% to the left of my parent (the container).

.original-relative-div {

I am the original position of the relative div before I was given "position: relative;"

This is where I would sit inside my parent container (in the normal flow of the document).

If the above div was not positioned absolute I would be sitting pushed beneath the pink line.

.fixed-div {
 position: fixed;
top: 20%;
right: 5%;

this lil div will always chill here, stuck to this position on screen because it is fixed 20% from the top and 5% from the right of the screen.
